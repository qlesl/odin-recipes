French Toast Recipe

Start your morning with a warm and comforting plate of Classic French Toast. Each slice of thick bread is soaked in a rich mixture of eggs, milk, vanilla, and a hint of cinnamon, then cooked to golden perfection with a crispy exterior and soft, fluffy center. This easy breakfast favorite can be topped with sweet maple syrup, a dusting of powdered sugar, or fresh fruit for a delicious and customizable treat. Perfect for a cozy weekend brunch or a quick weekday pick-me-up, French toast is a timeless dish that always satisfies!

Ingredients:
  • 4 slices of thick bread (preferably brioche or challah)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter (for cooking)
  • Maple syrup, powdered sugar, or fresh fruit (for serving)
Instructions:
  1. Whisk the Batter: In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, sugar (if using), and a pinch of salt until well combined.
  2. Soak the Bread: Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a little butter. Dip each slice of bread into the egg mixture, ensuring both sides are well coated. Let the bread soak for a few seconds to absorb the mixture.
  3. Cook the French Toast: Place the soaked bread slices on the hot skillet and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown and slightly crispy.
  4. Serve: Serve your French toast warm, topped with maple syrup, a sprinkle of powdered sugar, or fresh fruit like berries or banana slices.

Enjoy your delicious French toast breakfast!
